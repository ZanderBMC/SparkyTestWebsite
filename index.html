Electrical FAQ for Homeowners and Facility Managers

Detailed answers to the most common questions we receive about safety, pricing, and how our service works.

When should I call an emergency electrician instead of waiting for standard business hours?

Treat any situation that involves heat, smoke, burning smells, visible arcing, or repeated tripping of the main switch as urgent. If you can see flames or there is immediate danger to people or property, call 000 first, then contact us on 1300 555 247. For non life threatening issues such as partial loss of power, a hot switch, or repeated tripping of a single circuit, turn the affected circuit off at the switchboard and call us for priority same day attendance. Leaving faults unattended can damage appliances and increase fire risk, so it is safer to get advice as soon as you notice something unusual.

How do you price electrical work and what does upfront pricing mean?

Before we start any work, our electrician inspects the site, confirms the exact scope, and explains the available options. You are given a clear written quote that includes labor, materials, and any required compliance certificates. There are no call out surprises or hidden weekend multipliers added at the end. If additional issues are discovered during the job, they are discussed and priced separately so you stay in control of the final invoice. For larger projects such as full switchboard upgrades or commercial fit outs, we can break the work into staged options with separate line items.

What electrical work must be done by a licensed electrician in NSW?

In NSW, any fixed wiring work must be carried out by a licensed electrician working for a registered contractor. This includes installing or relocating power points, lights, switches, ovens, hot water circuits, air conditioning circuits, switchboard upgrades, EV chargers, and new circuits of any kind. DIY work on fixed wiring is not only unsafe, it can void insurance and make it difficult to sell or lease a property later. Simple tasks such as plugging in appliances, changing light bulbs, or resetting a safety switch are usually fine for owners to do, but if you are ever unsure, it is safer to speak to a licensed professional before touching the switchboard.

How often should my home or business have an electrical safety check?

For residential properties, we recommend a comprehensive electrical safety check every five years, or before you purchase a home, renovate, or add large new loads such as EV chargers or ducted air conditioning. For businesses, strata buildings, and facilities with higher duty cycles, it is wise to schedule inspections every one to three years, depending on usage and regulatory requirements. Our safety checks include testing of safety switches, inspection of switchboards and earthing, sampling of outlets and lighting circuits, and a documented report outlining any hazards, code issues, or energy saving opportunities.

Can you help with both residential and commercial electrical projects?

Yes. Apex Electrical supports single family homes, townhouses, strata complexes, retail stores, warehouses, offices, and light industrial sites. We understand that households prioritise safety, comfort, and budget, while commercial clients often need staged works, after hours scheduling, and integration with facility management plans. Our team can coordinate with builders, strata managers, and other trades to minimise downtime and keep projects running to schedule.

What can I expect on the day of my appointment?

On the day of your booking, you receive an SMS or call when the electrician is on the way, along with a clear arrival window. Our team arrives in uniform, carries ID, and walks through the job with you before any tools come out. Drop sheets and shoe covers are used where required, and work areas are cleaned before we leave. At completion, the electrician tests all circuits, demonstrates how any new equipment operates, and emails certificates or compliance documents for your records.

The Apex Electrical Story

A local business built around one simple goal - treat every home as if it were our own.

Apex Electrical began when our founder saw too many families left in the dark by rushed jobs, poor communication, and trades that never showed when they said they would. After more than a decade on the tools across residential, commercial, and industrial sites, he decided to build a company that combined old fashioned manners with modern standards.

From day one we focused on three non negotiables: respect for your time, obsessive attention to safety, and workmanship that still looks good ten years after we leave. That means clear quoting, honest advice, and electricians who take the time to label boards, explain what has been done, and leave every work area clean and tidy.

Today Apex Electrical supports homes, strata buildings, and businesses across NSW, but the mindset has not changed. We still answer the phone locally, we still send real electricians to assess your job, and we still measure success by how confident and safe you feel when the power comes back on.
